# Policyholder Digital Experience Site

Your policyholders need access to view and make changes to their account, policies, claims, and billing information. The Policyholder Digital Experience template and components get you started in building a custom Digital Experience site that meets those needs.

**Where:** The Policyholder site is styled with the Newport Design System. If you have a Vlocity Insurance License, you can access the template and components that comprise this feature.

You get the Policyholder Digital Experience template by installing the [Insurance FSC Unmanaged Package](https://help.salesforce.com/s/articleView?id=ind.insurance_t_install_the_insurance_fsc_unmanaged_package_650038.htm&language=en_US&type=5 "To start using templates to create an Experience site for your users, install the Insurance FSC unmanaged package."). To learn how, see .

**Who:** Your Vlocity developers configure the Policyholder site. Policyholders you've granted credentials to can log on to the site.

**Why:** The Policyholder site gives your policyholders the ability to view important information about their account, including:

-   Policy details
    
-   Open claims status
    
-   Recent account activity
    
-   Billing status
    
-   Recent transactions
    
-   Insured items details
    
-   Contact information for their agent
    

You can also empower your policyholders to manage their account preferences and policies. With the Policyholder site, your policyholders can manage their account by doing these tasks:

-   View billing info
    
-   Update contact methods
    
-   Print ID cards
    
-   Change their address
    

At the policy level, your Policyholder site users can:

-   Create claims
    
-   Modify their coverage
    
-   Change their billing address
    
-   Additional options dependent upon the type of policy
    
    Example: Add or remove drivers and vehicles on an auto policy.
    

We left it simple because we expect you to customize it to make it your own.

**How:** We provide a template and a set of components to get you started. But we expect that you'll want to customize the components we provide and possibly create your own.

Here's an overview of how you use the template and components to build your own Policyholder site:

1.  Install the Insurance FSC unmanaged package to get access to the Policyholder Experience template.
    
    [Install the Insurance FSC Unmanaged Package](https://help.salesforce.com/s/articleView?id=ind.insurance_t_install_the_insurance_fsc_unmanaged_package_650038.htm&language=en_US&type=5 "To start using templates to create an Experience site for your users, install the Insurance FSC unmanaged package.")
    
2.  Create a Policyholder site for your users.
    
    [Create Your Experience Site](https://help.salesforce.com/s/articleView?id=ind.insurance_t_create_your_experience_site_650072.htm&language=en_US&type=5 "To start using the templates provided in the unmanaged package, create an Experience site for your users.")
    
3.  In the Experience Builder, save your own custom version of the Insurance Policy Holder template.
    
    [Save Your Experience Site Template](https://help.salesforce.com/s/articleView?id=ind.insurance_save_your_experience_site_template_650229.htm&language=en_US&type=5 "Save a template so that you can customize the Experience site for your users. This step is necessary so that you can uninstall the unmanaged package.")
    
4.  Customize the Policyholder site components to fit your brand and your users' needs. Create your own components if you need them.
    
    [Digital Experience Site Component Customization Overview](https://help.salesforce.com/s/articleView?id=ind.insurance_digital_experience_site_component_customization_overview_649514.htm&language=en_US&type=5 "We've provided out-of-the-box components for Insurance Experience sites, but we know you'll probably want to brand them with your logos, icons, colors, and fonts for your customers to see. You can also customize the data that's displayed.")
    
5.  Using your custom template and custom components, build your Policyholder site.
    
    [Add Customized Components to the Site](https://help.salesforce.com/s/articleView?id=ind.insurance_add_customized_components_to_the_site_649927.htm&language=en_US&type=5 "When you customize Experience components (or create new ones), you need to add them to your experience site so your end users can see them.")
    
6.  When it's ready, publish your Policyholder site and start granting your policyholders access to it.
