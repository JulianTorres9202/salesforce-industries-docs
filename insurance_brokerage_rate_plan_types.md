# Rate Plan Types

Rate plans typically fall into specific types that determine premium calculations.

Here are the rate plan types.

| Type | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Fully Insured Health | Represents premium-based health related coverages such as medical, dental, and vision. |
| PPPM (Per Person Per Month) | Represents a fee-based service such as Administrative Service Only (ASO) policies and Employee Assistance Programs (EAP). |
| PMPM (Per Member Per Month) | Represents fee-based services such as Administrative Service Only (ASO) policies and Employee Assistance Programs (EAP). |
| PEPM (Per Employee Per Month) | Represents fee-based services such as Administrative Service Only (ASO) policies and Employee Assistance Programs (EAP). |
| Flat Fee Based (Irrespective to number of employees) | Represents fee-based services such as Administrative Service Only (ASO) policies and Employee Assistance Programs (EAP). |
| Benefit Volume | Represents the sum of coverages for all employees. This premium-based rate plan type is used for Life Insurance, Accidental Death & Dismemberment (AD&D), and Short Term and Long Term Disability |
| Covered Payroll | Represents the sum of payroll of all employees. This premium-based rate plan type is used for Life Insurance, AD&D, and Disability. Employees are usually covered up to their payroll amount under this type of coverage. |
| Fully Insured Equivalent | Estimates the premium equivalent to calculate the brokerage commissions. |

## Rate Plan Line Item

A rate tier is represented by a line item on a rate plan. The number of people enrolled in each tier determines the total premium for that tier.
