# Group Census Management

A Group Census is a central concept in group insurance. It represents a snapshot of all eligible members of a group (such as employees of an employer) and their dependents. Insurance carriers use census data at every stage of the group benefits lifecycle, including quoting, rating, and enrollment.

What a group census contains

-   Group information – The account the census is linked to, effective date, and census type (quote vs. enrollment).
-   Member details – Employee or member personal information. For example, date of birth, gender, smoker status, and dependents.
-   Summaries – Aggregate statistics such as total members, dependents, or members by class that aligns with quotes and contracts.
